About Me

I am a maître de conférences ( ≈ lecturer / associate professor) in the Laboratoire de Mathématiques Nicolas Oresme at the Université de Caen‐Normandie. My research deals mainly with connections between representation theory and geometry, often motivated by combinatorial phenomena appearing in cluster theory and related areas.

I previously held an EPSRC Postdoctoral Fellowship at the Universities of Glasgow and Leeds, a position at the Institut für Algebra und Zahlentheorie at Universität Stuttgart, and a fellowship at the Max‐Planck‐Institut für Mathematik in Bonn. I obtained my PhD from the University of Bath, where I was advised by Alastair King.

I am one of the administrators of the FDLIST, a mailing list and community for representation theorists with an interest in finite‐dimensional algebras, and in particular maintain the list of workshops, conferences and seminars.

More information about my research and teaching can be found by following the links in the header. Some of this information is summarised in my CV (although this is often less up‐to‐date than my webpage).
